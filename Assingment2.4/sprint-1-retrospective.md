# Sprint 1 Retrospective

Date: 2026-05-13
Participants: Thapelo Magwai (Full-Stack), Caitlin Ngwana (Backend), Thato Madida (Frontend), Nido Maphosa (Scrum Master)

## Start

These are things the team should start doing in Sprint 2 that were not done in Sprint 1.

Before committing a story that is rated 5 points or more, the team should do a short investigation called a spike. This means spending one day looking at the technical details before the sprint starts so there are no surprises during execution. Story 9 would have benefited from this.

The team should also start chasing external dependencies before the sprint begins. The email credentials from IT should have been requested the week before the sprint, not discovered as a problem on Day 2.

The Scrum Master should read through the Definition of Done for each story out loud during sprint planning so everyone is clear on exactly what done means before the sprint starts.

## Stop

These are things the team should stop doing because they caused problems in Sprint 1.

The team should stop estimating stories without reading the Technical Notes carefully. Story 9 had an audit trail requirement written in the Technical Notes that was overlooked during estimation. That one requirement added a full day of extra work.

The team should stop waiting to raise concerns until they become confirmed problems. The Story 9 risk was visible on Day 3 but was only raised on Day 5. That cost two days of response time.

The team should stop treating external dependencies as one person's problem. The email integration affected the whole team's ability to sign off on Story 1 but it was treated as only Caitlin's concern.

## Continue

These are things that worked well and should be kept in Sprint 2.

The daily standup at 09:00 with the Yesterday, Today, Blockers format worked well. Every meeting was short and everyone stayed aligned throughout the sprint.

Running frontend and backend work at the same time saved a lot of time. Thato building the UI while Caitlin built the API meant the two pieces were ready to connect quickly.

Agreeing on the database schema on Day 1 before writing any code was a good decision. It stopped the team from having to go back and change things later.

Getting stories into the In Review column early was also good. Stories 1 and 3 were in review by Day 4 which gave the team time to act on feedback before the sprint ended.
